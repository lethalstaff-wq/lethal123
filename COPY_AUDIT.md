# BRAND VOICE RULES — Lethal Solutions

Ten rules. Every writer (and every prompt) must pass these before shipping copy.

1. **Blacklist the SaaS lexicon.** No "unlock", "seamless", "revolutionize", "elevate", "empower", "solutions", "ecosystem", "next-level", "premium", "world-class". If you typed one, delete the sentence and start over.

2. **Headlines: 1–5 words, verb-first when possible.** "Ship with us." "Cart's dry." "Read the manual." "Press play." "What changed." Never two-line headlines unless the second line is three words or fewer.

3. **Body copy: cut 30–50%. Then cut 10% more.** If a sentence doesn't add a fact, a number, or a joke, delete it.

4. **Proof > adjectives.** Never say "fast." Say "<60s." Never say "trusted." Say "234 days undetected." Never say "24/7 support." Say "Discord reply in 4 minutes."

5. **Gamer voice, not marketer voice.** Readers queue Valorant and open Discord tickets. They don't "embark on a journey." Use: "queue clean", "stay invisible", "burn the HWID", "the squad", "close the tab", "banked", "stacked."

6. **Empty states and 404s earn a line.** This is where brand voice shows. "404. Page got banned." "Cart's dry." "Empty shelf." Never "Oops! Something went wrong."

7. **Buttons are verbs. Two words max.** "Claim code", "Find it", "Resend to inbox", "Go window shopping", "Pick a seat", "Track order." Never "Click here", "Learn more", "Get started" alone.

8. **Stat rows must be specific and verifiable.** No "24/7", no "99%+", no "Happy customers". Yes: "8,700 keys shipped", "<2h patch-to-fix", "234 days undetected", "Discord reply in 4 min."

9. **Confidence without peacocking.** Don't write "the best", "the most advanced", "the industry-leading". Lethal either is the standard or it isn't — show, don't claim. Security brags like "256-bit encryption" read defensive on a page that should feel casual.

10. **Every page has one sentence that sounds like a person, not a company.** Even on /faq or /track. Example: "16+ only. We check." "No meetings. Ever." "Some reviews are angry. They're still here." If the page reads like it was approved by a legal team, it's dead.

Wherever they fell back on marketing-page conventions (referrals program, apply hero, reviews intro), the copy collapses into generic SaaS. Rewriting to the rules above should lift the site to 8+ average.
